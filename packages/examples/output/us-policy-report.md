## Recommendations

1. **Modify: Double NIH Research Funding** — Probable causal relationship (Grade B), welfare impact: 75.0
   - Priority score: 56.38; Policy impact score: 0.75
   - Previous NIH doubling (1998-2003) led to genomic revolution. ROI on biomedical research estimated at 40-50%.
   - Current: $48B annual NIH budget → Recommended: $96B annual NIH budget
   - Blocking factors: budget constraint
   - Causal confidence: High (0.75)
2. **Modify: Earned Income Tax Credit Expansion** — Strong causal evidence (Grade A), welfare impact: 60.0
   - Priority score: 43.69; Policy impact score: 0.73
   - Strong RCT evidence shows EITC expansion reduces poverty, increases labor force participation, and improves child outcomes.
   - Current: $7,430 max credit (family w/ 3+ children) → Recommended: $10,000 max credit with expanded childless worker eligibility
   - Blocking factors: budget constraint, political
   - Causal confidence: Very High (0.81)
3. **Enact: Permanent Expanded Child Tax Credit** — Probable causal relationship (Grade B), welfare impact: 55.0
   - Priority score: 38.18; Policy impact score: 0.69
   - 2021 expansion cut child poverty by 46%. Canada CDB shows sustained poverty reduction effects.
   - Blocking factors: budget constraint, political
   - Causal confidence: High (0.79)
4. **Enact: Universal Pre-K (Ages 3-4)** — Probable causal relationship (Grade B), welfare impact: 55.0
   - Priority score: 36.52; Policy impact score: 0.66
   - Perry Preschool and Head Start RCTs show long-term gains in earnings, education, and reduced crime. ROI estimated at 7:1.
   - Blocking factors: budget constraint, political
   - Causal confidence: High (0.74)
5. **Modify: Medicare Drug Price Negotiation Expansion** — Probable causal relationship (Grade B), welfare impact: 50.0
   - Priority score: 33.38; Policy impact score: 0.67
   - All peer OECD nations negotiate drug prices. Cross-country evidence shows 30-60% savings with no reduction in innovation.
   - Current: 10 drugs negotiated in 2026 → Recommended: 50+ drugs by 2028, covering Part D and Part B
   - Blocking factors: political
   - Causal confidence: High (0.78)
6. **Enact: National Paid Family Leave** — Probable causal relationship (Grade B), welfare impact: 45.0
   - Priority score: 28.94; Policy impact score: 0.64
   - US is only OECD nation without federal paid leave. State programs (CA, NJ, WA) show improved maternal/infant health and labor force participation.
   - Blocking factors: budget constraint, political
   - Causal confidence: High (0.77)
7. **Modify: High-Skill Immigration Reform** — Probable causal relationship (Grade B), welfare impact: 45.0
   - Priority score: 26.77; Policy impact score: 0.59
   - Canada and Australia points-based systems show high-skill immigration boosts GDP per capita and innovation.
   - Current: 85,000 H-1B annual cap → Recommended: 200,000+ H-1B cap with startup visa category
   - Blocking factors: political
   - Causal confidence: High (0.69)
8. **Modify: Sustained Infrastructure Investment (IIJA+)** — Probable causal relationship (Grade B), welfare impact: 40.0
   - Priority score: 24.05; Policy impact score: 0.60

   - Current: IIJA $550B new spending (2022-2026) → Recommended: Sustained $150B/year above baseline
   - Blocking factors: budget constraint, political
   - Causal confidence: High (0.74)
9. **Enact: National Clean Energy Standard** — Probable causal relationship (Grade B), welfare impact: 40.0
   - Priority score: 23.76; Policy impact score: 0.59

   - Causal confidence: High (0.72)
10. **Enact: Federal Incentives for Zoning Reform** — Probable causal relationship (Grade B), welfare impact: 40.0
   - Priority score: 22.67; Policy impact score: 0.57

   - Causal confidence: High (0.73)

   - Priority score: 16.53; Policy impact score: 0.47
   - Portugal decriminalization (2001) shows reduced overdose deaths, HIV, and incarceration without increased use.

   - Blocking factors: political, federal preemption
   - Causal confidence: Moderate (0.55)
13. **Enact: Occupational Licensing Reform** — Probable causal relationship (Grade B), welfare impact: 27.0
   - Priority score: 13.81; Policy impact score: 0.51
   - Licensing reform in AZ and universal recognition states show increased labor mobility and lower consumer prices.

14. **Enact: NEPA & Permitting Reform** — Possible association (Grade C), welfare impact: 25.0
   - Priority score: 10.80; Policy impact score: 0.43

   - Blocking factors: political
   - Causal confidence: Moderate (0.55)
15. **Repeal: War on Drugs / Mandatory Minimums** — Weak evidence (Grade D), welfare impact: -30.0
   - Priority score: 9.52; Policy impact score: 0.32
   - US incarceration rates rose 500% since 1970 with no decline in drug use. Portugal decriminalization (2001) reduced overdose deaths and HIV with no usage increase.
   - Current: Federal mandatory minimums for drug possession/distribution → Recommended: Repeal mandatory minimums; redirect to treatment (Portugal model)
   - Blocking factors: political
   - Causal confidence: Low (0.33)
16. **Modify: Military Spending Efficiency Audit & Reform** — Possible association (Grade C), welfare impact: 17.0
   - Priority score: 5.68; Policy impact score: 0.33
   - GAO estimates $100-200B in potential savings. Allied nations achieve comparable readiness at lower cost.
   - Current: DoD has failed 6 consecutive audits → Recommended: Clean audit by 2028, 5% efficiency savings
   - Blocking factors: political, implementation capacity
   - Causal confidence: Moderate (0.44)
17. **Repeal: Abstinence-Only Sex Education** — Weak evidence (Grade D), welfare impact: -10.0
   - Priority score: 2.31; Policy impact score: 0.23
   - Multiple RCTs (Mathematica 2007, Trenholm et al.) show no effect on sexual behavior. States with abstinence-only programs have higher teen pregnancy rates.
   - Current: $85M annual federal abstinence education funding → Recommended: Redirect to evidence-based comprehensive sex education
   - Blocking factors: political
   - Causal confidence: Low (0.32)
18. **Repeal: Ethanol Fuel Mandate (Renewable Fuel Standard)** — Weak evidence (Grade D), welfare impact: -3.0
   - Priority score: 1.49; Policy impact score: 0.21
   - Lifecycle analysis shows negligible net emissions reduction. Raises food prices by 5-15% (corn diverted to fuel). Primarily benefits corn lobby, not environment.
   - Current: 15B gallons/year corn ethanol mandate → Recommended: Phase out corn ethanol mandate; maintain cellulosic RFS only
   - Blocking factors: political
   - Causal confidence: Low (0.31)
19. **Repeal: Penny Coin Continuation** — Weak evidence (Grade D), welfare impact: -1.0
   - Priority score: 0.16; Policy impact score: 0.16
   - Costs 3x face value to mint ($179M net loss in 2023). Canada (2013) and Australia (1992) eliminated lowest denomination coins with no economic disruption.
   - Current: ~7B pennies minted annually at 3.07 cents each (2023) → Recommended: Eliminate penny; round cash transactions to nearest 5 cents
   - Blocking factors: political
   - Causal confidence: Low (0.26)
