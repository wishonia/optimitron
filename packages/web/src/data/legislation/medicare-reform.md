## SECTION 4. KEY PROVISIONS.

### SUBSECTION 401. ESTABLISHMENT OF THE NATIONAL HEALTH SAVINGS & CATASTROPHIC INSURANCE SYSTEM (NHSACIS).

(a) **STRUCTURE.** The existing fragmented healthcare financing mechanisms shall be replaced by a three-tiered system modeled on Singapore's 3M Health System:
    (1) **Universal Health Savings Accounts (MediSave America):** Mandatory, portable, interest-bearing personal health savings accounts.
    (2) **National Catastrophic Health Insurance (MediShield America):** Universal, lifelong catastrophic health insurance.
    (3) **National Health Safety Net Fund (MediFund America):** A means-tested safety net for individuals unable to meet healthcare expenses.

(a) **CURRENT OVERSPEND.** The United States currently overspends on healthcare by a factor of 3.3x compared to the efficient floor, with per-capita spending at $10,333 versus an efficient floor of $3,142. This represents an annual overspend of $7,191 per capita ($10,333 - $3,142).

(b) **TOTAL POTENTIAL ANNUAL SAVINGS.** Based on a U.S. population of approximately 341.7 million [6](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQG2cQyRXFYNISYxsahHlDybojRUK7x_fsAneHfJG-0ygsY_6ldLZzIv19-WcHy4Cfi-Ytg65n5Vnjzpn3CR7xk8id5qmRWO9yR0T84r0AN02OsFLAUOMP9RDgMo9nvxx4KhIe7-gwczKZMizHpGO3iAqPm4ki_59rakmA==), the total potential annual savings from moving towards the efficient spending floor would be:
    341,700,000 population * $7,191/capita = $2,457,484,700,000 (approximately $2.46 trillion).

(c) **PROJECTED NET SAVINGS FROM KEY PROVISIONS.**
    (1) **MediSave America (Administrative & Efficiency Gains):** $50 billion annually.
    (2) **MediShield America (Administrative & Efficiency Gains):** $150 billion annually.
    (3) **MediFund America (Consolidation & Efficiency Gains):** $75 billion annually.
    (4) **Price Transparency Mandate (Market Efficiency Gains):** $95 billion annually.
    (5) **Hospital Competition Reforms (Operational Efficiency):** $200 billion annually.
    (6) **Pragmatic Clinical Trials Initiative (Research Efficiency):** $16 billion annually.

(d) **TOTAL AGGREGATE NET SAVINGS.** Sum of projected net savings from key provisions = $50 + $150 + $75 + $95 + $200 + $16 = $586 billion annually.
This $586 billion represents an initial, conservative estimate of annual savings achievable through the outlined reforms, a significant step toward the $2.46 trillion potential.

## SECTION 6. UNIVERSAL DIVIDEND POOL & CITIZEN CHOICE.

(a) **ESTABLISHMENT OF UNIVERSAL DIVIDEND POOL.** All net savings generated from the provisions of this Act shall be consolidated into a "Universal Dividend Pool" within the U.S. Treasury. This pool shall be distributed equally to every adult citizen of the United States as a monthly payment.

(b) **PROJECTED PER-PERSON ANNUAL DIVIDEND.** Based on total aggregate net savings of $586 billion and an adult U.S. population of approximately 258 million, the projected annual dividend per adult citizen is:
    $586,000,000,000 / 258,000,000 adults = $2,271 per adult per year.
    This translates to approximately $189 per adult per month.

(c) **CITIZEN CHOICE PROVISION.**
    (1) **OPT-IN REDIRECTION:** Every citizen receiving the Universal Dividend shall be provided a clear, accessible option to redirect any portion of their monthly payment to any federal budget category of their choosing (e.g., Defense, Healthcare, Education, Infrastructure, Research).
    (2) **DEFAULT:** The default option shall be to receive the full cash payment.
    (3) **INFRASTRUCTURE:** The Department of the Treasury, in coordination with the Internal Revenue Service, shall establish an online portal (e.g., TreasuryDirect.gov) and integrate this option into annual tax filing processes (similar to the Presidential Election Campaign Fund checkbox). Citizens shall be able to change their allocation quarterly.
    (4) **TRANSPARENCY:** Annual reports shall be published detailing the aggregate citizen redirection choices for each federal budget category, providing real-time revealed preference data on public spending priorities.

These two metrics shall be the sole ultimate measures of the Act's success.

### SUBSECTION 802. REVIEW CADENCE.

The GAO shall conduct a biennial review of the Act's performance, commencing two years after enactment. Every two years, the GAO shall re-measure both metrics established in Section 801 and compare them to the baseline measurements. The results of these reviews shall be published publicly and submitted to Congress.

### SUBSECTION 803. TARGET THRESHOLDS.

(a) **EXPECTED IMPROVEMENTS.** The Act aims for the following improvements:
    (1) **Real After-Tax Median Household Income:** An annual growth of +0.5 percentage points (pp) in real after-tax median household income.
    (2) **Median Healthy Life Expectancy at Birth:** An increase of +0.3 years in median healthy life expectancy at birth per year.
    These targets project a +2.5 pp income growth and +1.5 years HALE increase within 5 years of full implementation.

(b) **MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE THRESHOLDS.** If, by the second biennial review (Year 4 after enactment), either of the following conditions is met, the Act shall be considered to have failed to meet minimum acceptable thresholds:
    (1) **Real After-Tax Median Household Income:** Growth is less than 50% of the expected target (i.e., less than +0.25 pp annual growth).
    (2) **Median Healthy Life Expectancy at Birth:** Increase is less than 50% of the expected target (i.e., less than +0.15 years annual increase).

### SUBSECTION 804. HARD SUNSET PROVISION.

If, by the second biennial review (Year 4 after enactment), BOTH the Real After-Tax Median Household Income and the Median Healthy Life Expectancy at Birth metrics fail to meet their MINIMUM acceptable thresholds as defined in Section 803(b), then this Act shall automatically expire, be repealed, and all associated programs and funding shall cease, unless reauthorized by a supermajority vote (two-thirds majority) in both chambers of Congress. This provision ensures accountability and prevents the perpetuation of ineffective legislation.

### SUBSECTION 805. DATA COLLECTION MANDATE.

To facilitate rigorous evaluation, the following agencies shall be mandated to collect, analyze, and publish the necessary data annually, ensuring public accessibility:
    (a) **Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) / U.S. Census Bureau:** Real after-tax median household income.
    (b) **Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) / National Center for Health Statistics (NCHS):** Median healthy life expectancy at birth.
    (c) **Office of Management and Budget (OMB):** Per-capita healthcare spending, disaggregated by major categories.
    (d) **Department of Health and Human Services (HHS):** Data on average procedure costs, preventive care visits, avoidable emergency room visits, medical debt-related bankruptcies, delayed treatments, uncompensated care costs, access to essential medical services, hospital length of stay, patient satisfaction scores, and healthcare-associated infections.
    All data shall be made publicly available in machine-readable formats.

### SUBSECTION 806. BENCHMARK JURISDICTIONS.

The GAO review shall explicitly benchmark the United States' performance against the following five OECD countries: Singapore, Israel, Switzerland, Japan, and Australia. If these benchmark countries continue to outperform the United States on both Real After-Tax Median Household Income and Median Healthy Life Expectancy at Birth despite the reforms enacted by this Act, the biennial review must include a detailed analysis of the reasons for this disparity and recommend specific policy adjustments to close the gap.
