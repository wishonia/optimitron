### SECTION 3. PURPOSE.
The purpose of this Act is to strategically reorient federal spending, specifically by reducing inefficient military expenditure, and reallocating the resulting savings into evidence-based programs designed to directly improve the health, economic well-being, and life expectancy of all United States citizens. This Act aims to achieve health outcomes comparable to top-performing nations such as Spain (life expectancy of 82.87 years) while reducing per-capita military spending from its current $2052 toward an efficient floor of $279.

#### Subtitle A — Military Spending Optimization

**SEC. 401. STRATEGIC DEFENSE SPENDING REALIGNMENT.**

(a) **REPLACES:** Broad, untargeted military expenditures and administrative overhead within the Department of Defense (DOD).
(b) **CURRENT COST:** An estimated $687.3 billion annually based on current per-capita spending of $2052 (assuming a US population of 335 million).
(c) **NEW COST:** An estimated $93.465 billion annually, aligning with the efficient floor spending of $279 per capita.
(d) **NET SAVINGS:** Approximately $593.835 billion annually. These savings, along with all other net savings from this Act, shall flow to the Universal Dividend Pool established under Section 405.
(e) **What it does (Modeled on top-performing OECD nations):** This provision mandates a phased reduction and restructuring of military spending to align the United States with the per-capita defense spending efficiency observed in high-performing OECD countries, such as Ireland, Spain, and Austria, which achieve national security goals with significantly lower budgetary outlays relative to their population and national outcomes. The realignment will focus resources on core defense capabilities, technological superiority, and personnel welfare, while significantly curtailing wasteful procurement, redundant administrative layers, and inefficient overseas commitments.
(f) **How it works operationally:**
    (1) The Secretary of Defense, in consultation with the Office of Management and Budget (OMB), shall submit a plan to Congress within 180 days of enactment detailing a 4-year phased reduction in military expenditure to reach the $279 per-capita spending floor.
    (2) The plan shall identify specific areas for reduction, including but not limited to, the consolidation of overseas bases, reduction of non-essential civilian and contractor personnel, streamlining of procurement processes, and elimination of programs failing to demonstrate cost-effectiveness in enhancing national security.
    (3) Funds released from these reductions shall be immediately transferred to the Universal Dividend Pool.
(g) **Expected impact (Cite evidence):** A reallocation of $593.835 billion in annual overspend is projected to free up significant federal resources, enabling reinvestment in domestic priorities that directly improve life expectancy and economic prosperity. This recalibration is expected to maintain national security while fostering economic stability and growth.
(h) **Implementation Timeline:** Phased reduction plan over four (4) fiscal years, commencing 180 days after enactment.
