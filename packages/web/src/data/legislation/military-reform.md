#### Subtitle D — Investment in Pragmatic Clinical Trials

**SEC. 331. NATIONAL INSTITUTE FOR HEALTH RESEARCH (NIHR) AMERICA.**

(a)  **WHAT IT DOES:** There is hereby established the "National Institute for Health Research (NIHR) America," a new agency within the Department of Health and Human Services, dedicated to funding and conducting pragmatic clinical trials. This initiative is modeled on the United Kingdom's NIHR, which produces actionable evidence at a fraction of the cost of traditional U.S. research models.

(b)  **HOW IT WORKS OPERATIONALLY:**
    (1)  **FUNDING ALLOCATION:** A dedicated portion of the reallocated military savings shall be appropriated annually to NIHR America, with a focus on trials that address common health conditions, compare existing treatments, and are integrated into routine clinical practice.
    (2)  **RESEARCH PRIORITIES:** NIHR America shall prioritize research questions directly relevant to improving population health outcomes, reducing healthcare costs, and enhancing the efficiency of the American 3M Health System.
    (3)  **COLLABORATION:** NIHR America shall foster collaboration between academic medical centers, community healthcare providers, and patient advocacy groups to ensure research is relevant, ethical, and rapidly translatable into practice.
    (4)  **EFFICIENT MODEL:** The NIHR America will adopt a streamlined bureaucratic structure, focusing on rapid review, efficient resource allocation, and timely dissemination of findings to maximize impact and cost-effectiveness.

(c)  **EXPECTED IMPACT:** By shifting a portion of health research funding towards pragmatic clinical trials, NIHR America is expected to generate actionable evidence at significantly lower costs, leading to more effective and efficient healthcare practices and improved patient outcomes across the American 3M Health System. This contrasts with the current U.S. system where less than 10% of NIH's $48B/year budget goes to pragmatic trials.

(d)  **IMPLEMENTATION TIMELINE.**
    (1)  **Year 1-2:** Establishment of NIHR America, appointment of leadership, development of funding mechanisms, and initiation of initial pilot research calls.
    (2)  **Year 3-5:** Expansion of research portfolio, establishment of national research networks, and integration of findings into clinical guidelines and practice.

### SECTION 4. FISCAL IMPACT.

(a)  **ESTIMATED SAVINGS FROM MILITARY SPENDING REDUCTION.** Based on current per-capita military spending of $2052 and an efficient floor of $279, the United States currently overspends by $1773 per capita. With a population of approximately 330 million, this represents an annual overspend of approximately $585 billion. The phased reduction outlined in Section 301 aims to reallocate a significant portion of these savings, moving towards the efficient floor and generating hundreds of billions of dollars in available funds for domestic investment over the next decade.

(b)  **ALLOCATION OF SAVINGS.** The savings realized from the reduction in military spending shall be directly reallocated as follows:
    (1)  **AMERICAN 3M HEALTH SYSTEM:** A substantial portion shall be dedicated to establishing and funding the initial endowment for Medifund America, subsidizing MediShield America premiums for low-income individuals, and building the necessary administrative and regulatory infrastructure for the American 3M Health System.
    (2)  **AMERICAN BASIC INCOME PROGRAM:** A significant portion shall be allocated to fund the American Basic Income Program, ensuring sufficient capital for regular, unconditional payments to eligible citizens.
    (3)  **NATIONAL INSTITUTE FOR HEALTH RESEARCH AMERICA:** Dedicated funding shall be provided to establish and operate NIHR America, promoting efficient, evidence-based health research.
