### SECTION 3. PURPOSE

The purpose of this Act is to enhance the overall health and economic well-being of United States citizens by reforming inefficient social security spending mechanisms, drawing upon evidence-based approaches from top-performing OECD countries. Specifically, this Act aims to achieve healthy life expectancy outcomes comparable to those in South Korea (83.57 years) and Israel (82.17 years), while reducing the per-capita social spending of the United States from its current $12,848 towards the efficient floor of $6,427, thereby generating substantial savings that will be directly returned to the American people.

### SECTION 7. EVALUATION AND SUNSET PROVISIONS

#### SUBSECTION 701. BASELINE MEASUREMENT

Within 90 days of the enactment of this Act, the Government Accountability Office (GAO) shall establish and publish baseline measurements for the following metrics:
    (a) **Real After-Tax Median Household Income**: Sourced from the United States Census Bureau (Census) and the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) Current Population Survey (CPS).
    (b) **Median Healthy Life Expectancy at Birth (HALE)**: Sourced from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) and the National Center for Health Statistics (NCHS).

#### SUBSECTION 702. REVIEW CADENCE

The Government Accountability Office (GAO) shall conduct a biennial review of the Act's impact. Every two (2) years following the establishment of baseline measurements, the GAO shall re-measure both the real after-tax median household income and the median healthy life expectancy at birth. The results of these biennial reviews, including comparisons to baseline measurements and target thresholds, shall be published publicly on the GAO's official website and presented to relevant Congressional committees.

#### SUBSECTION 703. TARGET THRESHOLDS

The following specific improvements are expected and shall serve as target thresholds for evaluation:
    (a) **Real After-Tax Median Household Income Growth**: An average increase of at least +0.5 percentage points per year in real after-tax median household income growth over the baseline within five (5) years of enactment.
    (b) **Median Healthy Life Expectancy at Birth (HALE) Increase**: An increase of at least +0.3 years in median healthy life expectancy at birth within five (5) years of enactment.

The minimum acceptable thresholds shall be 50% of the expected target thresholds:
    (a) **Minimum Income Growth**: An average increase of at least +0.25 percentage points per year in real after-tax median household income growth.
    (b) **Minimum HALE Increase**: An increase of at least +0.15 years in median healthy life expectancy at birth.

#### SUBSECTION 704. HARD SUNSET

If, by the second biennial review (occurring approximately four (4) years after enactment), **BOTH** the real after-tax median household income and the median healthy life expectancy at birth fail to meet their respective **MINIMUM acceptable thresholds** as defined in Section 703, then this Act shall automatically expire, be repealed, and cease to have force or effect on the date of the public release of that second biennial review, unless explicitly renewed by a supermajority vote (two-thirds majority in both chambers) of the United States Congress within 180 days of that date. This provision is designed to prevent the continuation of ineffective legislation.

#### SUBSECTION 705. DATA COLLECTION MANDATE

The following federal agencies are hereby mandated to collect, analyze, and publish relevant data annually, ensuring public accessibility for all data required for the evaluation provisions of this Act:
    (a) **Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS)**: Annually publish data on real after-tax median household income and related economic indicators.
    (b) **Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) / National Center for Health Statistics (NCHS)**: Annually publish data on median healthy life expectancy at birth, preventable hospitalizations (AHRQ PQI), preventive care visits (BRFSS), and chronic disease prevalence (NHANES).
    (c) **Office of Management and Budget (OMB)**: Annually publish detailed per-capita social security spending data, including healthcare and social welfare expenditures.
    (d) **Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS)**: Establish and maintain the National Healthcare Price Database and annually publish data on average procedure costs and intervention uptake.
    (e) **National Institute for Pragmatic Research (NIPR)**: Annually publish reports on the number of pragmatic trials funded, research-to-practice translation times, and cost-effectiveness analyses of interventions.
    (f) **Department of the Treasury / Internal Revenue Service (IRS)**: Annually publish data on Universal Dividend distribution and aggregate citizen allocation choices from the Citizen Choice Provision.

#### SUBSECTION 706. BENCHMARK JURISDICTIONS

For ongoing comparative analysis and to inform future adjustments, the biennial reviews conducted by the GAO shall include an explicit comparison of the United States' performance on real after-tax median household income and median healthy life expectancy at birth against the following five (5) OECD benchmark countries:
    (1) South Korea
    (2) Israel
    (3) New Zealand
    (4) Switzerland
    (5) Japan

If these benchmark countries consistently outperform the United States on both metrics despite the reforms enacted by this Act, the GAO review must explain the reasons for this continued divergence and recommend specific policy, regulatory, or institutional adjustments to address the identified gaps.
