## DRAFT MODEL LEGISLATION: THE AMERICAN HEALTH EFFICIENCY AND UNIVERSAL DIVIDEND ACT OF 2026

**A Bill**

To improve the efficiency and outcomes of the United States healthcare system (excluding Medicare and Medicaid, which shall remain separate programs unless explicitly integrated by future legislation), to reduce per-capita health spending, and to establish a Universal Dividend Pool funded by the resulting savings for direct distribution to American citizens.

---

### SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "American Health Efficiency and Universal Dividend Act of 2026."

### SECTION 3. PURPOSE.

The purpose of this Act is to establish a modernized, efficient, and equitable healthcare financing system for the United States, targeting components outside of the existing Medicare and Medicaid programs. This Act aims to achieve health outcomes comparable to the best-performing OECD countries, specifically Israel and Portugal, while reducing overall per-capita healthcare spending from $10,333 toward the efficient floor of $3,142. By replacing inefficient existing mechanisms with proven, evidence-based approaches, this Act will generate substantial savings to be returned directly to American citizens, thereby improving both health and economic well-being.

### SECTION 4. KEY PROVISIONS.

#### Subtitle A — American Health Savings and Catastrophic Coverage System

##### SEC. 401. ESTABLISHMENT OF AMERICAN HEALTH SAVINGS ACCOUNTS (AHSAS).

(a) **What it does:** Modeled on Singapore's Medisave system, this provision establishes mandatory, portable, and tax-advantaged Health Savings Accounts for all working Americans, replacing a significant portion of current fragmented private health insurance premiums and out-of-pocket spending for routine care. [5](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQE0NwwTQWf_TJ1CF0sVu3xsa_LZ9WuINxlOEwEmfs4z23BghwmvOhj71UlSamW4stG45BNvPM3CMKFV2XyD-f57Eky8lPIx9qNUWvH0g-TRHWuq9_9AxTAio6JtB-T266Ne5-vYZLf2G6JyxRa3DUKkJAlrLgUAdvM=)

(b) **How it works operationally:**
    (1) **Mandatory Contributions:** All employers and employees shall contribute a percentage of wages (initially set at 8%, with 4% from employer and 4% from employee) into individual, interest-bearing AHSA accounts. Contributions shall be tax-deductible for employees and tax-exempt for employers. The Secretary of the Treasury, in consultation with the Secretary of Health and Human Services, shall annually review and adjust contribution rates to ensure long-term solvency and adequacy, not to exceed 10% of wages.
    (2) **Permitted Uses:** Funds in AHSAs can be used by the account holder and their immediate family members for a wide range of approved medical expenses, including primary care visits, outpatient specialist consultations, prescription drugs (with co-payment), preventive care services, mental health services, and certain inpatient services. Restrictions on withdrawals shall apply to ensure funds are available for future and retirement medical needs, similar to Singapore's Medisave withdrawal limits. [5](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQE0NwwTQWf_TJ1CF0sVu3xsa_LZ9WuINxlOEwEmfs4z23BghwmvOhj71UlSamW4stG45BNvPM3CMKFV2XyD-f57Eky8lPIx9qNUWvH0g-TRHWuq9_9AxTAio6JtB-T266Ne5-vYZLf2G6JyxRa3DUKkJAlrLgUAdvM=)
    (3) **Portability:** AHSAs are fully portable, remaining with the individual regardless of employment status.
    (4) **Investment Options:** Account holders shall be offered a range of investment options for their AHSA funds, subject to federal regulation, to allow for growth.
    (5) **Price Transparency Mandate:** All healthcare providers, including hospitals and clinics, shall be required to publish clear, comprehensive, and machine-readable prices for all items and services, including health plan-specific negotiated prices, on their websites. This includes a patient-friendly list for "shoppable" services. The Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) shall enforce these requirements, with non-compliant providers facing civil monetary penalties.

(c) **Expected Impact:**
    (1) **Intermediate Metric: Price Transparency → Reduction in Average Procedure Cost:** Implementation of robust price transparency mandates (Sec. 401(b)(5)) is expected to drive a **30% reduction in average procedure costs** in the non-Medicare/Medicaid market within 5 years, as patients, empowered by information, shop for care and providers compete on price and quality. (Measurable via CMS price database).
    (2) **Intermediate Metric: AHSAs/Transparency → Increase in Preventive Care Visits:** The direct financial incentive of using personal savings (AHSAs) combined with transparent pricing is expected to lead to a **15% increase in preventive care visits** within 3 years. (Measurable via CDC Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS) survey).
    (3) **Intermediate Metric: Increased Prevention → Reduction in Preventable Hospitalizations:** This increase in preventive care and early intervention is expected to result in a **20% reduction in preventable hospitalizations** (Ambulatory Care Sensitive Conditions) within 5 years. (Measurable via Agency for Healthcare Research and Quality (AHRQ) Prevention Quality Indicators (PQI)).
    (4) **Ultimate Metric: Health Outcomes (HALE):** These improvements in access to affordable preventive care and reduced preventable hospitalizations are expected to contribute to an increase of **+0.3 years in median healthy life expectancy at birth** within 5 years. (Measurable via CDC/NCHS).
    (5) **Ultimate Metric: Economic Well-being (Income):** Reduced healthcare spending due to efficiency gains will redirect resources, contributing to an estimated **+$2,100/year increase in real after-tax median household income**. (Measurable via Census Bureau Current Population Survey (CPS) Annual Social and Economic Supplement).

(d) **Implementation Timeline:**
    *   **Year 1:** Establishment of AHSA framework, regulatory bodies, and initial enrollment. Mandatory price transparency enforcement begins.
    *   **Year 2-3:** Full implementation of mandatory contributions and broader use of AHSA funds. Pilot programs for provider competition and quality reporting.
    *   **Year 4-5:** Refinement of AHSA policies, expansion of covered services, and full realization of price transparency and competition impacts.

(e) **Replacement Framing:**
    *   **REPLACES:** The current fragmented and inefficient employer-sponsored and individual private health insurance markets, as well as a significant portion of current out-of-pocket healthcare expenditures that do not contribute to long-term health.
    *   **CURRENT COST:** A substantial portion of the estimated $2.4 trillion in annual national health expenditures not covered by Medicare/Medicaid, representing the overspend in the non-governmental sector.
    *   **NEW COST:** The collective cost of mandatory AHSA contributions (employer and employee portions) and administrative overhead of the new system.
    *   **NET SAVINGS:** The difference between the current inefficient spending and the new, more efficient spending under the AHSA system.
    *   **SAVINGS DESTINATION:** ALL net savings flow to the Universal Dividend Pool.

##### SEC. 402. ESTABLISHMENT OF NATIONAL CATASTROPHIC HEALTH INSURANCE (AMERICAShield Life).

(a) **What it does:** Modeled on Singapore's MediShield Life, this provision establishes a universal, mandatory catastrophic health insurance program to protect all Americans from large, unforeseen medical bills, regardless of pre-existing conditions. [5](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQE0NwwTQWf_TJ1CF0sVu3xsa_LZ9WuINxlOEwEmfs4z23BghwmvOhj71UlSamW4stG45BNvPM3CMKFV2XyD-f57Eky8lPIx9qNUWvH0g-TRHWuq9_9AxTAio6JtB-T266Ne5-vYZLf2G6JyxRa3DUKkJAlrLgUAdvM=)

(b) **How it works operationally:**
    (1) **Universal Coverage:** Every legal resident of the United States shall be automatically enrolled in AmericaShield Life, providing lifelong protection against high-cost, acute inpatient and certain expensive outpatient treatments.
    (2) **Financing:** AmericaShield Life shall be funded through premiums, adjusted based on income and age, with significant government subsidies for lower-income individuals. Premiums can be paid partially from AHSA accounts.
    (3) **Cost-Sharing:** The program shall feature deductibles and co-insurance to encourage prudent healthcare utilization, preventing over-consumption while ensuring comprehensive protection for truly catastrophic events. These cost-sharing amounts shall be calibrated to avoid undue burden on lower-income populations, consistent with Singapore's calibration notes. [5](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQE0NwwTQWf_TJ1CF0sVu3xsa_LZ9WuINxlOEwEmfs4z23BghwmvOhj71UlSamW4stG45BNvPM3CMKFV2XyD-f57Eky8lPIx9qNUWvH0g-TRHWuq9_9AxTAio6JtB-T266Ne5-vYZLf2G6JyxRa3DUKkJAlrLgUAdvM=)
    (4) **Scope of Coverage:** AmericaShield Life primarily covers large hospital bills, long-term treatments, and other high-cost medical events. It is explicitly designed to complement, not replace, primary and routine care funded by AHSAs.

(c) **Expected Impact:**
    (1) **Intermediate Metric: Catastrophic Coverage → Reduction in Medical Bankruptcies:** Universal catastrophic coverage is expected to result in a **90% reduction in personal medical bankruptcies** within 5 years. (Measurable via federal bankruptcy court data).
    (2) **Intermediate Metric: Financial Protection → Increase in Elective/Delayed Care:** Reduced fear of catastrophic costs will enable patients to seek necessary but often delayed non-emergency care, leading to a **20% increase in utilization of elective surgical or diagnostic procedures** currently delayed due to cost concerns. (Measurable via claims data for common elective procedures).
    (3) **Ultimate Metric: Health Outcomes (HALE):** Reduced financial stress and improved access to necessary specialized care are expected to contribute to an additional **+0.2 years in median healthy life expectancy at birth** within 7 years. (Measurable via CDC/NCHS).
    (4) **Ultimate Metric: Economic Well-being (Income):** Elimination of medical debt and financial ruin will directly preserve household wealth, contributing to an additional **+$1,000/year increase in real after-tax median household income**. (Measurable via Census Bureau Current Population Survey (CPS) Annual Social and Economic Supplement).

(d) **Implementation Timeline:**
    *   **Year 1-2:** Design of AmericaShield Life premium structure and benefit package. Development of IT infrastructure.
    *   **Year 3:** Phased enrollment and launch, beginning with automatic enrollment of all citizens.
    *   **Year 4-5:** Full operationalization and ongoing adjustments based on claims data and actuarial analysis.

(e) **Replacement Framing:**
    *   **REPLACES:** The fragmented and often inadequate catastrophic coverage provided by existing private insurance plans, which often leave individuals vulnerable to high out-of-pocket costs and medical debt.
    *   **CURRENT COST:** The societal costs associated with medical bankruptcies, uncompensated care, and the administrative overhead of navigating a complex and often insufficient catastrophic coverage landscape.
    *   **NEW COST:** The collective cost of AmericaShield Life premiums (after government subsidies) and administrative overhead.
    *   **NET SAVINGS:** The difference, including avoided societal costs.
    *   **SAVINGS DESTINATION:** ALL net savings flow to the Universal Dividend Pool.

##### SEC. 403. ESTABLISHMENT OF AMERICAN HEALTH SAFETY NET (AMERICAFund).

(a) **What it does:** Modeled on Singapore's MediFund, this provision creates a permanent, means-tested safety net to ensure no American is denied necessary medical care due to inability to pay, even after exhausting AHSA and AmericaShield Life benefits. [5](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQE0NwwTQWf_TJ1CF0sVu3xsa_LZ9WuINxlOEwEmfs4z23BghwmvOhj71UlSamW4stG45BNvPM3CMKFV2XyD-f57Eky8lPIx9qNUWvH0g-TRHWuq9_9AxTAio6JtB-T266Ne5-vYZLf2G6JyxRa3DUKkJAlrLgUAdvM=)

(b) **How it works operationally:**
    (1) **Endowment Fund:** AmeriFund shall be established as a federal endowment fund, with an initial capitalization from the Universal Dividend Pool and subsequent allocations as needed, to generate ongoing returns for financial assistance.
    (2) **Means-Tested Assistance:** Eligibility for AmeriFund assistance shall be determined through a rigorous means-testing process, focusing on individuals and families below a specified income threshold (e.g., 200% of the federal poverty line) who have exhausted their AHSA funds and met their AmericaShield Life deductibles and co-insurance.
    (3) **Payer of Last Resort:** AmeriFund shall serve as the payer of last resort for approved medical expenses at public and private healthcare institutions, ensuring access to essential care for the most vulnerable.

(c) **Expected Impact:**
    (1) **Intermediate Metric: Safety Net → Reduction in Uncompensated Care:** AmeriFund will significantly reduce the burden of uncompensated care on hospitals and healthcare providers, leading to an estimated **50% reduction in uncompensated care costs** within 3 years. (Measurable via hospital financial reports to CMS).
    (2) **Intermediate Metric: Financial Security → Improved Health-Seeking Behavior for Low-Income:** The presence of a robust safety net will remove financial barriers for low-income populations, leading to a **25% increase in timely health-seeking behavior** (e.g., visiting a doctor for symptoms, not just emergencies) among this group. (Measurable via CDC BRFSS survey data for low-income demographics).
    (3) **Ultimate Metric: Health Outcomes (HALE):** Improved access to care for the most vulnerable is expected to contribute to an additional **+0.1 years in median healthy life expectancy at birth** within 5 years, particularly impacting health equity. (Measurable via CDC/NCHS, disaggregated by socioeconomic status).
    (4) **Ultimate Metric: Economic Well-being (Income):** Reduced financial burden from medical costs on low-income households will improve their economic stability, contributing to an additional **+$500/year increase in real after-tax median household income** for this demographic. (Measurable via Census Bureau Current Population Survey (CPS) Annual Social and Economic Supplement).

(d) **Implementation Timeline:**
    *   **Year 1-2:** Establishment and capitalization of the AmeriFund. Development of eligibility criteria and administrative processes.
    *   **Year 3:** Full launch of AmeriFund assistance programs.
    *   **Year 4-5:** Ongoing review and adjustment of fund allocation and eligibility to meet evolving needs.

(e) **Replacement Framing:**
    *   **REPLACES:** Existing fragmented state and local charity care programs, and the implicit subsidy provided by emergency rooms for uninsured and underinsured care, which are often inefficient and inconsistent.
    *   **CURRENT COST:** The estimated billions in uncompensated care costs absorbed by the healthcare system and indirectly by taxpayers and insured individuals.
    *   **NEW COST:** Annual disbursements from the AmeriFund endowment and administrative costs.
    *   **NET SAVINGS:** The difference, primarily in avoided uncompensated care and administrative overhead of current fragmented safety nets.
    *   **SAVINGS DESTINATION:** ALL net savings flow to the Universal Dividend Pool.

#### Subtitle B — Universal Dividend Pool and Citizen Choice

##### SEC. 411. ESTABLISHMENT OF THE UNIVERSAL DIVIDEND POOL.

(a) **What it does:** This provision establishes a Universal Dividend Pool to receive all net savings generated from the American Health Efficiency and Universal Dividend Act of 2026, and any subsequent efficiency reforms. These funds shall be distributed equally to every adult citizen of the United States.

(b) **How it works operationally:**
    (1) **Funding Mechanism:** All net savings identified in Section 401(e), 402(e), and 403(e) shall be deposited into the Universal Dividend Pool, administered by the Department of the Treasury.
    (2) **Distribution:** Beginning in Year 2 of this Act's implementation, and annually thereafter, the total annual balance of the Universal Dividend Pool shall be divided by the total adult population of the United States (estimated at 258 million adults for calculation purposes). This per-person amount shall be distributed in equal monthly payments to every adult citizen through direct deposit to their designated bank account or a secure federal debit card.

(c) **Projected Per-Person Annual Dividend Calculation:**
    *   Current US Health Spending (non-Medicare/Medicaid focus, based on overall per capita figure from prompt): $10,333/capita.
    *   Efficient Floor Spending: $3,142/capita.
    *   Potential Savings Per Capita: $10,333 - $3,142 = $7,191.
    *   Total US Population (estimate): 349,000,000. [1](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQGXYWuzseFo7EbuE9pL-hVNG-jkgCGFn8quefu9TBmf2jP7KQ4LKIDb4WCdUdrRzFz6Km4LDSorJi0HFOi4SMnLrwYoAPe3A2svtq8yjLvRWR-KZ0TkHt6B2vC5KUJlq0s=)
    *   Total Potential Annual Savings: $7,191/capita * 349,000,000 people = ~$2.51 trillion.
    *   Adult US Population (for dividend distribution, as per prompt example): 258,000,000.
    *   **Projected Per-Person Annual Dividend (full realization):** $2,510,000,000,000 / 258,000,000 adults = **~$9,728.68 per adult per year**, or approximately **$810.72 per adult per month**.

(d) **Expected Impact:**
    (1) **Intermediate Metric: Dividend Distribution → Reduction in Poverty Rate:** The direct cash transfer is expected to result in a **50% reduction in the child poverty rate** and a **30% reduction in the overall poverty rate** within 5 years. (Measurable via Census Bureau Supplemental Poverty Measure (SPM)).
    (2) **Intermediate Metric: Increased Income → Improved Social Determinants of Health:** Reduced financial insecurity is expected to lead to a **20% reduction in food insecurity** and a **15% increase in access to stable housing** for low-income households within 5 years. (Measurable via USDA Food Security Supplement to the Current Population Survey; HUD housing stability metrics).
    (3) **Ultimate Metric: Health Outcomes (HALE):** Improved social determinants of health are expected to contribute an additional **+0.2 years in median healthy life expectancy at birth** within 7 years. (Measurable via CDC/NCHS).
    (4) **Ultimate Metric: Economic Well-being (Income):** The Universal Dividend will directly increase disposable income, contributing an additional **+$9,728/year increase in real after-tax median income** (at full realization), primarily impacting the lower and middle-income brackets. (Measurable via Census Bureau Current Population Survey (CPS) Annual Social and Economic Supplement).

##### SEC. 412. CITIZEN CHOICE PROVISION.

(a) **Opt-in Redirection:** Every adult citizen receiving the Universal Dividend shall have the option to redirect any portion of their monthly payment to any federal budget category of their choosing, including but not limited to Defense, Education, Scientific Research, or existing healthcare programs.
(b) **Implementation:** This redirection mechanism shall be implemented through a secure online portal managed by the Department of the Treasury, similar to existing tax return checkboxes for campaign funds. Citizens may change their allocation quarterly.
(c) **Default:** The default shall be to receive the full cash payment directly.
(d) **Purpose:** To empower citizens with direct agency over how their tax dollars are spent, ensuring that reforms are supported by revealed preference rather than solely political mandates.

(c) **Savings Destination:** All net savings shall be transferred to the Universal Dividend Pool, as detailed in Section 411.

##### SEC. 703. TARGET THRESHOLDS.

(a) **Expected Improvements:** The expected improvements within five (5) years of full implementation (Year 5, following a 2-year initial phase) are:
    (1) **Real after-tax median household income growth:** +0.5 percentage points per year average growth.
    (2) **Median healthy life expectancy at birth:** +0.3 years within 5 years.
(b) **Minimum Acceptable Thresholds:** The minimum acceptable thresholds for this Act to remain in effect are 50% of the expected improvements defined in subsection (a).

##### SEC. 704. HARD SUNSET.

If **BOTH** the real after-tax median household income and the median healthy life expectancy at birth fail to meet their respective MINIMUM acceptable thresholds by the second biennial review (Year 4 of implementation), this Act shall automatically expire unless renewed by a supermajority vote (60% of both chambers) of the United States Congress.

    (i) **Housing stability metrics:** Annually from the Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD).

##### SEC. 706. BENCHMARK JURISDICTIONS.

The GAO's biennial review shall include a comparative analysis against the following benchmark OECD countries: Singapore, Israel, Portugal, and the Czech Republic. If these benchmark countries continue to outperform the United States on both real after-tax median household income and median healthy life expectancy at birth despite the reforms enacted by this Act, the review shall explain the reasons for this divergence and recommend specific adjustments to the legislation.

### SECTION 8. EVIDENCE APPENDIX.

(a) **Singapore's 3M Health System (Medisave / MediShield / Medifund):**
    (1) **Universal Health Financing:** Singapore achieved universal health coverage through a three-tier financing system implemented since 1984. This system emphasizes individual responsibility, market mechanisms, and a robust safety net. [5](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQE0NwwTQWf_TJ1CF0sVu3xsa_LZ9WuINxlOEwEmfs4z23BghwmvOhj71UlSamW4stG45BNvPM3CMKFV2XyD-f57Eky8lPIx9qNUWvH0g-TRHWuq9_9AxTAio6JtB-T266Ne5-vYZLf2G6JyxRa3DUKkJAlrLgUAdvM=)
    (2) **Medisave (Mandatory Health Savings Accounts):** Singaporeans contribute 8-10.5% of their wages to Medisave accounts, which are tax-exempt and earn interest. These funds cover routine medical expenses and can be used for family members. This encourages prudent spending and personal ownership of health costs. [5](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQE0NwwTQWf_TJ1CF0sVu3xsa_LZ9WuINxlOEwEmfs4z23BghwmvOhj71UlSamW4stG45BNvPM3CMKFV2XyD-f57Eky8lPIx9qNUWvH0g-TRHWuq9_9AxTAio6JtB-T266Ne5-vYZLf2G6JyxRa3DUKkJAlrLgUAdvM=)
    (3) **MediShield Life (National Catastrophic Insurance):** A universal and mandatory insurance scheme protects against large hospital bills and certain expensive outpatient treatments. It features deductibles and co-insurance, with premiums subsidized based on income, ensuring affordability. [5](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQE0NwwTQWf_TJ1CF0sVu3xsa_LZ9WuINxlOEwEmfs4z23BghwmvOhj71UlSamW4stG45BNvPM3CMKFV2XyD-f57Eky8lPIx9qNUWvH0g-TRHWuq9_9AxTAio6JtB-T266Ne5-vYZLf2G6JyxRa3DUKkJAlrLgUAdvM=)
    (4) **Medifund (Safety Net):** A government endowment fund provides a means-tested safety net for the poorest citizens who cannot afford their medical bills even after Medisave and MediShield Life. It acts as a payer of last resort. [5](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQE0NwwTQWf_TJ1CF0sVu3xsa_LZ9WuINxlOEwEmfs4z23BghwmvOhj71UlSamW4stG45BNvPM3CMKFV2XyD-f57Eky8lPIx9qNUWvH0g-TRHWuq9_9AxTAio6JtB-T266Ne5-vYZLf2G6JyxRa3DUKkJAlrLgUAdvM=)
    (5) **Market Mechanisms and Cost Control:** Despite significant government involvement (e.g., owning most hospitals), Singapore actively uses competition and co-payments to control costs. Hospitals are mandated to publish prices and quality indicators, enabling informed patient choice and fostering market competition. This system has resulted in world-class health outcomes at significantly lower costs (e.g., 4% of GDP) compared to countries like the United States. [5](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQE0NwwTQWf_TJ1CF0sVu3xsa_LZ9WuINxlOEwEmfs4z23BghwmvOhj71UlSamW4stG45BNvPM3CMKFV2XyD-f57Eky8lPIx9qNUWvH0g-TRHWuq9_9AxTAio6JtB-T266Ne5-vYZLf2G6JyxRa3DUKkJAlrLgUAdvM=)

(b) **Cost Control and Outcome Efficiency in Other Top-Performing Countries:**
    (1) **Portugal:** Employs a universal, tax-funded public National Health Service (SNS) with small co-payments for most services, and exemptions for vulnerable populations. General Practitioners act as gatekeepers to specialized care, strengthening primary care.
    (2) **Czech Republic:** Features a universal public healthcare system funded predominantly by mandatory health insurance contributions, with the government covering vulnerable groups. It uses a heavily regulated system with government monitoring of expenditures, DRG systems for hospitals, and price regulation for pharmaceuticals.
    (3) **Israel:** Provides universal coverage through national health insurance funded by an income-related health tax and general revenues. Citizens choose from four competing non-profit health plans, which offer a mandated benefit package with some cost-sharing. The Ministry of Health regulates fees and sets drug prices based on international averages.

(c) **Cross-Cutting Themes for Efficiency:** Common themes across these successful systems include:
    *   **Universal Coverage:** Ensures access to care for all, often preventing costly emergency-only treatments.
    *   **Shared Responsibility:** A blend of individual savings/contributions, insurance, and government subsidies.
    *   **Cost-Sharing Mechanisms:** Deductibles and co-payments encourage mindful consumption of healthcare services.
    *   **Price and Quality Transparency:** Empowers patients and fosters competition among providers.
    *   **Strong Primary Care Emphasis:** Reduces reliance on more expensive specialist and hospital care for preventable conditions.
    *   **Centralized Regulation of Pricing:** Government oversight for drug prices and service fees prevents arbitrary increases.
