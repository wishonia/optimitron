The purpose of this Act is to:
    (1) Achieve scientific and innovation outcomes comparable to top-performing OECD countries, specifically Ireland, while reducing per-capita spending from the current $1,991 toward the efficient floor of $843.

**Subtitle C: Universal Welfare Reform and Citizen Dividend Consolidation**

(a) **Universal Welfare Consolidation (Modeled on Efficiency Principles):**

    (2) **How it works operationally:**
        (A) **Program Merger:** Within 24 months, all identified federal welfare programs will be merged into a single "Department of Citizen Support Services," eliminating redundant administrative structures, eligibility determinations, and reporting requirements.
        (B) **Administrative Savings:** The new department will focus on efficient administration, direct assistance for specialized needs not covered by the dividend (e.g., severe disability care), and job training/placement services.
        (C) **Poverty Line Reference:** While the Universal Dividend itself is not tied to the poverty line, the combined impact of the dividend and remaining targeted support aims to ensure no citizen falls below a nationally defined poverty threshold.

        (C) **Improved Food Security:** A projected 15% decrease in household food insecurity rates. (Measurable: USDA Food Security Survey).
        (D) **Causal Chain:** Consolidation of welfare programs and administrative streamlining + Universal Dividend distribution → reduced administrative waste + increased household economic security → decreased poverty rate and food insecurity + reduced stress-related health issues → improved overall well-being and productivity → **+0.1 years median HALE** (measurable: CDC/NCHS) and **+$500/yr after-tax median income** (measurable: Census CPS).
