### SECTION 3. PURPOSE.

The purpose of this Act is to achieve healthcare outcomes comparable to the Czech Republic, specifically an infant survival rate (inverted mortality) of 97.6, while reducing per-capita healthcare spending in the United States from its current level of $10,333 toward the efficient floor of $2,911.

### SECTION 4. KEY PROVISIONS.

#### Subtitle A—Healthcare Financing Reform: The American Medisave & Shield Act

**SEC. 401. ESTABLISHMENT OF THE AMERICAN MEDISAVE SYSTEM.**

(a) **REPLACES:** Portions of current federal and state Medicaid administrative overhead and uncompensated care costs.
(b) **CURRENT COST:** A significant portion of the total estimated annual overspend in the U.S. healthcare system, proportional to the administrative and uncompensated care replaced.
(c) **NEW COST:** Administrative costs for managing Medisave accounts, projected to be substantially lower than replaced costs.
(d) **NET SAVINGS:** To be calculated and transferred to the Universal Dividend Pool.
(e) **DESCRIPTION:** Establishes a mandatory, universal health savings account system (hereinafter referred to as "Medisave Accounts") for all United States citizens and legal residents, modeled on Singapore's Medisave.
(f) **OPERATIONAL MECHANISM:**
    (1) **MANDATORY CONTRIBUTIONS:** All employed individuals shall contribute 7.5% of their gross wages to their individual Medisave Account, with employers contributing an additional 2.5% of the employee's gross wages. Self-employed individuals shall contribute 10% of their net earnings. These rates are a calibrated adaptation of Singapore's 8-10.5% wage contributions. [18](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQGKeVo5ETq_SALpI07UysX4SEIL3gWwCLyE6oQTBbEJuuWEgeFODZOsLSvfExYQHmHn4hIvM6HJq8J8kx3qeXUUpIOGyeY0q0TEH80fhicJqzxYTHZ0i5EukeZDiiDLEjGNbH2NbSw=)
    (2) **FEDERAL CONTRIBUTION FOR VULNERABLE POPULATIONS:** The federal government shall contribute a means-tested amount to Medisave Accounts for individuals below 200% of the federal poverty line, funded by redirected existing federal health subsidy programs and Medicaid administrative savings. This replaces fragmented assistance with direct, portable savings.
    (3) **PERMITTED USES:** Funds in Medisave Accounts may be used for approved outpatient treatments, certain chronic disease management, approved vaccinations, and premiums for the American MediShield Life catastrophic insurance. Withdrawals for non-medical purposes shall be subject to a penalty of 50% and taxation as ordinary income.
    (4) **TAX-ADVANTAGED GROWTH:** Funds in Medisave Accounts shall grow tax-free.
(g) **EXPECTED IMPACT:** Based on Singapore's experience, Medisave encourages individual responsibility for routine healthcare expenses, promoting cost-consciousness and reducing unnecessary utilization. The mandatory savings ensure that individuals have dedicated funds for healthcare, preventing medical debt for common services.
(h) **IMPLEMENTATION TIMELINE:**
    (1) **YEAR 1:** Establish Medisave Account infrastructure, integrate with existing payroll systems. Pilot program for federal employees.
    (2) **YEARS 2-3:** Phased rollout for all citizens and legal residents, with public education campaigns. Mandatory contributions begin.
(i) **CAUSAL CHAIN & METRICS:**
    (1) **POLICY:** Mandatory Medisave Contributions.
    (2) **INTERMEDIATE METRIC 1:** **Increase in personal health savings balance.** (Measurable: Treasury Department, Medisave Account balances).
        *   **THRESHOLD:** 20% increase in average Medisave account balance over 3 years.
        *   **TRANSLATION TO HALE:** Higher personal savings reduce financial barriers to elective/preventive care, leading to earlier treatment and better long-term health outcomes.
        *   **TRANSLATION TO INCOME:** Reduced out-of-pocket spending on routine care, less medical debt, preserving household wealth.
    (3) **INTERMEDIATE METRIC 2:** **Reduction in non-emergency primary care visits paid via emergency rooms.** (Measurable: CMS claims data, state hospital associations).
        *   **THRESHOLD:** 25% reduction within 5 years.
        *   **TRANSLATION TO HALE:** More appropriate care settings lead to better health management and reduced complications.
        *   **TRANSLATION TO INCOME:** Lower costs for individuals and the system, reducing financial burden.

**SEC. 402. ESTABLISHMENT OF AMERICAN MEDISHIELD LIFE.**

(a) **REPLACES:** Existing federal and state catastrophic Medicaid and Medicare emergency coverage, as well as portions of private catastrophic insurance that are inefficiently priced.
(b) **CURRENT COST:** A significant portion of existing Medicaid, Medicare, and private catastrophic care spending.
(c) **NEW COST:** Premiums collected from Medisave Accounts and federal subsidies, actuarially determined for comprehensive catastrophic coverage.

#### Subtitle B—Universal Health & Opportunity Dividend

**SEC. 411. ESTABLISHMENT OF THE UNIVERSAL DIVIDEND POOL.**

(a) **DESCRIPTION:** All net savings generated from the provisions of this Act shall be consolidated into a single "Universal Dividend Pool."
(b) **DISTRIBUTION:** The total annual funds in the Universal Dividend Pool shall be distributed equally to every adult citizen of the United States (age 18 and older) as a monthly payment.
(c) **PROJECTED ANNUAL DIVIDEND:** Based on the reduction of per-capita spending from $10,333 to the efficient floor of $2,911 for the total U.S. population (estimated at 335 million), the total potential annual savings are approximately $2,486,370,000,000. Distributed among an adult U.S. population of approximately 258 million, the projected annual dividend per adult citizen is approximately $9,637.
(d) **CITIZEN CHOICE PROVISION:** Every citizen receiving the Universal Dividend may, through an opt-in mechanism on their Treasury account (similar to the Presidential Election Campaign Fund checkbox on tax returns), redirect any portion of their monthly payment to any federal budget category of their choosing (e.g., Defense, Healthcare, Education, Infrastructure, Research). The default option shall be to receive the full cash payment. Citizens may change their allocation quarterly via an online portal (e.g., IRS.gov or Treasury Direct). This provision aims to align individual preferences with public spending and provides a market-like feedback mechanism for government programs.

#### Subtitle C—National Institute for Evidence-Based Care

**SEC. 421. RESTRUCTURING OF HEALTHCARE RESEARCH TOWARDS PRAGMATIC CLINICAL TRIALS.**

(a) **REPLACES:** A portion of the current National Institutes of Health (NIH) budget that funds basic and early-phase research with limited immediate clinical applicability, specifically targeting areas where pragmatic trials offer higher return on investment.
(b) **CURRENT COST:** A portion of the current NIH budget ($48B/yr, with <10% on pragmatic trials).
(c) **NEW COST:** Reallocated NIH funds (e.g., 20% of current NIH budget, or $9.6B, dedicated to NIEC), with a focus on cost-efficient, actionable research.
(d) **NET SAVINGS:** Savings from more efficient research and resulting healthcare cost reductions shall flow to the Universal Dividend Pool.
(e) **DESCRIPTION:** Establishes the "National Institute for Evidence-Based Care" (NIEC), modeled on the United Kingdom's National Institute for Health and Care Research (NIHR), to prioritize and fund pragmatic clinical trials and evaluative research that directly informs policy and practice, particularly in areas related to healthcare efficiency and outcomes.
(f) **OPERATIONAL MECHANISM:**
    (1) **FOCUSED FUNDING PROGRAMS:** The NIEC shall establish funding programs for:
        *   **Applied Health and Social Care Research:** Addressing immediate health and care needs, including studies on improving infant survival and maternal health interventions.
        *   **Health Technology Assessment:** Evaluating the cost-effectiveness and efficacy of new medical devices, diagnostics, and health technologies in real-world settings.
        *   **Public Health Research:** Generating evidence for effective non-NHS (non-healthcare system) interventions to improve public health and reduce inequalities.
        *   **Research for Patient Benefit:** Funding studies on day-to-day practice to increase effectiveness and value for money in health and social care.
    (2) **OUTCOME-BASED FUNDING DISTRIBUTION:** NIEC funding for research delivery organizations (e.g., academic medical centers, public health departments, primary care networks) shall be allocated based on a formula that considers historic activity, research activity (weighted for complexity and number of open studies), and performance against key performance indicators aligned with government health priorities.     (3) **COLLABORATION MANDATE:** Requires collaboration between academia, industry, and healthcare delivery organizations (including public and private providers) in funded research projects.
(g) **EXPECTED IMPACT:** By shifting focus to pragmatic trials, the NIEC will produce actionable evidence at a fraction of the cost of traditional research, driving continuous improvement in healthcare delivery and policy, similar to the UK's NIHR model. ### SECTION 5. FISCAL IMPACT.

(a) **CURRENT SPENDING:** Based on the provided evidence, the United States spends $10,333 per capita on healthcare. With an estimated total U.S. population of 335,000,000, the total annual healthcare expenditure is approximately $3.46 trillion.
(b) **EFFICIENT FLOOR SPENDING:** The efficient floor spending for comparable outcomes is $2,911 per capita.
(c) **POTENTIAL SAVINGS PER CAPITA:** $10,333 - $2,911 = $7,422 per capita.
(d) **TOTAL PROJECTED ANNUAL SAVINGS:** $7,422 per capita * 335,000,000 people = approximately $2,486,370,000,000 ($2.49 trillion).
(e) **REALLOCATION:** All net savings shall flow into the Universal Dividend Pool for distribution to adult citizens, as detailed in Section 411.

The GAO shall conduct a biennial review of the implementation and impact of this Act. **SEC. 703. TARGET THRESHOLDS.**

The following specific improvements are expected:
    (a) **Real after-tax median household income:** An increase of +0.5 percentage points per year in income growth, compounded annually, over the baseline, within 5 years.

    (c) **Minimum acceptable thresholds:** If BOTH metrics fail to meet 50% of their respective target thresholds by the second review (Year 4 after enactment), the Act shall be subject to automatic expiration as described in Section 704.

**SEC. 704. HARD SUNSET.**

If, by the second biennial review (Year 4 after enactment), BOTH the real after-tax median household income and the median healthy life expectancy at birth fail to meet their MINIMUM acceptable thresholds as defined in Section 703(c), this Act shall automatically expire and all provisions herein shall cease to be in effect, unless renewed by a supermajority vote (two-thirds majority in both the House of Representatives and the Senate) of Congress.
