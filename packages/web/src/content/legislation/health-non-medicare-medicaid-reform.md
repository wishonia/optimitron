### SECTION 3. PURPOSE.

The purpose of this Act is to establish a comprehensive national health and economic well-being framework for the United States to achieve health outcomes comparable to Japan and South Korea, aiming to increase median healthy life expectancy at birth, while simultaneously reducing per-capita non-Medicare/Medicaid health spending from $10,333 towards the efficient floor of $3,588, and to enhance the economic well-being of all citizens through the redirection of efficiency savings.

#### Subtitle B—Universal Dividend and Reallocation

##### SEC. 411. ESTABLISHMENT OF THE UNIVERSAL DIVIDEND POOL.

(a) **What it does**: This provision establishes a Universal Dividend Pool into which all net savings from the reforms enacted by this Act shall flow. This pool will fund a monthly direct payment to every adult citizen, thereby directly returning efficiency gains to the populace.
(b) **How it works operationally**:
    (1) **Funding Mechanism**: All net savings identified in Subtitle A and other provisions of this Act shall be automatically transferred to the Universal Dividend Pool on a quarterly basis.
    (2) **Distribution**: The Secretary of the Treasury shall distribute an equal monthly payment to every adult citizen (aged 18 and over) of the United States.

### SECTION 7. EVALUATION & SUNSET PROVISIONS.

##### SEC. 701. BASELINE MEASUREMENT.

Within 90 days of enactment of this Act, the Government Accountability Office (GAO) shall, in coordination with relevant agencies, establish publicly accessible baseline measurements for:
    (a) **Real After-Tax Median Household Income**: As reported by the U.S. Census Bureau and the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS).
    (b) **Median Healthy Life Expectancy at Birth (HALE)**: As reported by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) and the National Center for Health Statistics (NCHS).

##### SEC. 702. REVIEW CADENCE.

The GAO shall conduct a biennial review of this Act's effectiveness. Every two years following the establishment of baseline measurements, the GAO shall re-measure both real after-tax median household income and median healthy life expectancy at birth, comparing them to the established baselines. The results of each review, along with a detailed analysis, shall be published publicly.

##### SEC. 703. TARGET THRESHOLDS.

The following target thresholds for improvement shall be established for the purposes of evaluating this Act:
    (a) **Real After-Tax Median Household Income**: A target of an average annual increase of +0.5 percentage points per year.
    (b) **Median Healthy Life Expectancy at Birth**: A target increase of +0.3 years within the first 5 years of full implementation.
    (c) **Minimum Acceptable Thresholds**: The minimum acceptable performance shall be defined as achieving 50% of the established target thresholds for both metrics.

##### SEC. 704. HARD SUNSET.

If, by the second biennial review (Year 4 after baseline establishment), **BOTH** the real after-tax median household income and the median healthy life expectancy at birth fail to meet their respective MINIMUM acceptable thresholds as defined in Section 703, this Act shall automatically expire and be repealed, unless renewed by a supermajority vote (60% of both chambers) of the United States Congress.

##### SEC. 705. DATA COLLECTION MANDATE.

To ensure robust evaluation, the following agencies shall be mandated to collect, analyze, and publish relevant data annually, ensuring public accessibility:
    (a) The Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) and the U.S. Census Bureau shall collect and publish data on median household income, adjusted for inflation and taxes.
    (b) The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) and the National Center for Health Statistics (NCHS) shall collect and publish data on healthy life expectancy at birth.
    (c) The Office of Management and Budget (OMB) shall collect and publish data on per-capita health spending (non-Medicare/Medicaid) and the administrative costs of all replaced welfare programs.
    (d) The National Health Savings and Catastrophic Coverage System (NHSC) shall collect and publish data on average procedure costs, preventive care visits, preventable hospitalizations, and medical debt-related bankruptcies.
    (e) The National Institute for Actionable Health Research (NIAHR) shall collect and publish data on pragmatic clinical trial output and the utilization of evidence-based treatments.
    (f) The USDA shall collect and publish data on food insecurity rates.

##### SEC. 706. BENCHMARK JURISDICTIONS.

Japan, South Korea, Australia, and Singapore are hereby designated as ongoing benchmark jurisdictions for the purposes of evaluating the effectiveness of this Act. If, during any biennial review, these benchmark countries continue to outperform the United States on both real after-tax median household income (where comparable data exists) and median healthy life expectancy at birth, the GAO review must include a detailed analysis of the reasons for the continued disparity and recommend specific legislative or administrative adjustments to address these gaps.

#### Subtitle C—Citizen Choice Provision

(a) **Rationale**: This provision directly addresses a major political objection to government spending reforms by empowering individual citizens with direct control over a portion of their tax dollars. By allowing citizens to redirect their Universal Dividend payments to federal budget categories, it introduces market-like revealed preference into government allocation. If a program is genuinely valued by citizens, they can voluntarily fund it; if not, the funds flow directly back to them, demonstrating a preference for the dividend over the inefficient program. This mechanism strengthens democratic accountability and public support for fiscal reforms.
