### SECTION 3. PURPOSE.

The purpose of this Act is to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of the education system of the United States to achieve student outcomes comparable to the best-performing countries globally, such as Singapore, while significantly reducing per-capita education spending from $2996 toward the efficient floor of $1288. Furthermore, this Act aims to reallocate all net savings generated by these reforms to a Universal Dividend Pool, thereby directly improving the real after-tax median household income and median healthy life expectancy at birth for all United States citizens.

**SEC. 405. UNIVERSAL DIVIDEND POOL AND CITIZEN CHOICE PROVISION.**

(a) **UNIVERSAL DIVIDEND POOL:** All net savings generated from the implementation of this Act, including savings from Subtitle A (Education Spending Efficiency and Reform) and Subtitle B (National Health Savings and Insurance System), shall be deposited into a dedicated "Universal Dividend Pool" within the Treasury.

(b) **DISTRIBUTION OF DIVIDEND:** The total annual amount in the Universal Dividend Pool shall be divided by the total adult population of the United States (estimated at 258,000,000 individuals). Each adult citizen shall receive an equal monthly payment from this pool.

(c) **CITIZEN CHOICE PROVISION:**
    (1) **Optional Redirection:** Any citizen receiving the Universal Dividend may, through an opt-in mechanism, redirect any portion of their monthly payment to any federal budget category of their choosing (e.g., Defense, Healthcare, Education, Infrastructure, Research).
    (2) **Default Payment:** The default shall be to receive the full cash payment directly.
    (3) **Implementation:** The Treasury Department, in collaboration with the Internal Revenue Service (IRS), shall establish an online portal (e.g., via IRS.gov or Treasury Direct) allowing citizens to select or modify their allocation preferences quarterly. This mechanism shall be analogous to the Presidential Election Campaign Fund checkbox on tax returns.

    (1) **Direct Economic Benefit:** Provides a direct, tangible financial benefit to every citizen, fostering broad public support for efficiency reforms.
    (2) **Increased After-Tax Income:** Directly increases the real after-tax median household income.
    (3) **Citizen Empowerment:** Empowers citizens with a direct say in federal spending priorities, aligning government allocation more closely with revealed preferences.

### SECTION 7. EVALUATION AND SUNSET PROVISIONS.

**SEC. 701. BASELINE MEASUREMENT.**

Within 90 days of the enactment of this Act, the Government Accountability Office (GAO) shall establish baseline measurements for the following two ultimate metrics:
    (1) **Real After-Tax Median Household Income:** To be established using data from the U.S. Census Bureau and the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS).
    (2) **Median Healthy Life Expectancy at Birth (HALE):** To be established using data from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) and the National Center for Health Statistics (NCHS).

**SEC. 702. REVIEW CADENCE.**

The GAO shall conduct a biennial review of the implementation and impact of this Act. Every two years from the date of enactment, the GAO shall re-measure the baseline metrics established in Section 701 and compare them to the baseline. The results of these reviews, including progress on all intermediate metrics outlined in Section 4, shall be published publicly.

**SEC. 703. TARGET THRESHOLDS.**

The following target thresholds are established for the ultimate metrics:
    (1) **Real After-Tax Median Household Income:** An average increase of +0.5 percentage points per year in income growth.
    (2) **Median Healthy Life Expectancy at Birth (HALE):** An average increase of +0.3 years within five years of full implementation of the Act.

Minimum acceptable thresholds are defined as 50% of these expected improvements.

**SEC. 704. HARD SUNSET.**

If, by the second biennial review (Year 4), **BOTH** the real after-tax median household income and the median healthy life expectancy at birth fail to meet their respective **MINIMUM** acceptable thresholds (as defined in Section 703), this Act shall automatically expire unless explicitly renewed by a supermajority vote (60% of members present and voting) in both chambers of Congress.

**SEC. 705. DATA COLLECTION MANDATE.**

The following agencies are mandated to collect, maintain, and publish, in a publicly accessible format, the data necessary for the evaluation of this Act:
    (1) **Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) and U.S. Census Bureau:** Median household income data, annually.
    (2) **Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) and National Center for Health Statistics (NCHS):** Median healthy life expectancy at birth data, annually.
    (3) **Office of Management and Budget (OMB):** Detailed per-capita and per-student education spending data (disaggregated by program and state), annually.
    (4) **Department of Education (DoED):** Data on teacher effectiveness ratings, instructional quality observations, teacher attrition rates, curriculum alignment scores, student engagement in problem-solving, PISA math proficiency levels, socioeconomic achievement gaps, access to advanced courses, and high school graduation rates (disaggregated by demographic groups), annually.
    (5) **Department of Health and Human Services (HHS):** CMS price database, BRFSS survey data on preventive care, AHRQ Prevention Quality Indicators (PQI) data, annually.

**SEC. 706. BENCHMARK JURISDICTIONS.**

For ongoing comparative analysis, the GAO shall continuously benchmark the United States' performance on the ultimate metrics against the following OECD countries:
    (1) Singapore
    (2) Japan
    (3) Taiwan (Chinese Taipei)
    (4) Switzerland
    (5) Estonia

If, during any biennial review, these benchmark countries continue to outperform the United States on both real after-tax median household income and median healthy life expectancy at birth, the GAO report shall include a detailed explanation of the potential reasons for this continued divergence and recommend specific legislative or administrative adjustments to this Act.
