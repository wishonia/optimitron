**SECTION 3. PURPOSE.**
The purpose of this Act is to achieve outcomes in public welfare and economic prosperity comparable to the top-performing OECD countries, particularly the Netherlands, while reducing per-capita science and technological spending from the current $1,991 towards the efficient floor of $1,064. Furthermore, this Act aims to reallocate the resultant savings to evidence-based programs that directly improve real after-tax median household income and median healthy life expectancy at birth for all American citizens.

      (A) **Mechanism:** The Secretary of the Treasury, in consultation with the Internal Revenue Service, shall establish a secure, opt-in mechanism on an individual's Treasury Direct account or a dedicated IRS.gov portal.

**SECTION 7. EVALUATION & SUNSET PROVISIONS.**

**(a) Baseline Measurement (Section X01).**
   Within 90 days of the date of enactment of this Act, the Government Accountability Office (GAO) shall establish baseline measurements for the following two metrics, which shall serve as the sole ultimate indicators of success for this Act:
   (1) **Real after-tax median household income:** Sourced from the United States Census Bureau's Current Population Survey (CPS) and the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS).
   (2) **Median healthy life expectancy at birth:** Sourced from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) and the National Center for Health Statistics (NCHS).

**(b) Review Cadence (Section X02).**
   The GAO shall conduct a comprehensive review and re-measurement of both baseline metrics established in Section 7(a) every two years following the date of enactment. The results of each biennial review, along with detailed analyses of progress towards target and minimum thresholds, shall be published publicly on a dedicated government website and presented to the relevant committees of Congress.

**(c) Target Thresholds (Section X03).**
   The following target and minimum acceptable thresholds for improvement are hereby established:
   (1) **Real after-tax median household income:**
      (A) **Expected Improvement:** An average annual growth of 0.5 percentage points per year for the first 5 years, for a cumulative increase of 2.5 percentage points over 5 years compared to baseline.
      (B) **Minimum Acceptable Improvement:** An average annual growth of 0.25 percentage points per year for the first 5 years, for a cumulative increase of 1.25 percentage points over 5 years compared to baseline.
   (2) **Median healthy life expectancy at birth:**
      (A) **Expected Improvement:** An increase of 0.3 years within 5 years compared to baseline.
      (B) **Minimum Acceptable Improvement:** An increase of 0.15 years within 5 years compared to baseline.

**(d) Hard Sunset (Section X04).**
   If, by the second biennial review (occurring in Year 4 after enactment), both the real after-tax median household income and the median healthy life expectancy at birth fail to meet their respective MINIMUM acceptable thresholds as defined in Section 7(c)(1)(B) and 7(c)(2)(B), then this Act shall automatically expire and all provisions shall cease to have force or effect, unless renewed by a supermajority vote (three-fifths of the members of each chamber) of the United States Congress.

**(e) Data Collection Mandate (Section X05).**
   The following agencies are hereby mandated to collect, standardize, and publicly publish, at least annually, the data necessary for the evaluation metrics specified in this Act:
   (1) **Median income data:** Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) and the Census Bureau, through the Current Population Survey (CPS).
   (2) **Healthy life expectancy data:** Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) and the National Center for Health Statistics (NCHS).
   (3) **Per-capita spending data (federal science/tech, healthcare, UBI, pragmatic trials):** Office of Management and Budget (OMB) and relevant federal agencies (NSF, NASA, HHS, NIH).
   All data collected under this section shall be made publicly accessible in machine-readable formats on a dedicated government portal.

**(f) Benchmark Jurisdictions (Section X06).**
   The GAO shall, as part of its biennial review, compare the performance of the United States on the two ultimate metrics (real after-tax median household income and median healthy life expectancy at birth) against the following benchmark jurisdictions: the Netherlands, Norway, Denmark, and Singapore. If these benchmark countries consistently outperform the United States on both metrics despite the reforms enacted by this Act, the GAO review must include a detailed analysis explaining the reasons for the disparity and recommend specific adjustments to the Act's provisions, policies, regulations, or institutional structures based on identified best practices in the benchmark jurisdictions.
