The purpose of this Act is to achieve educational outcomes comparable to the best-performing nations, specifically targeting PISA Math Scores equivalent to Singapore, while reducing per-capita education spending in the United States from its current level of $2,996 towards the efficient floor of $1,288.     (5)  **Efficient Resource Allocation → Ultimate Metric (Income)**: +$100/yr after-tax median household income within 5 years (measurable: Census CPS/BLS).
(h)  **IMPLEMENTATION TIMELINE**:

## SECTION 7. EVALUATION AND SUNSET PROVISIONS.

#### SEC. 701. BASELINE MEASUREMENT.
Within 90 days of enactment, the Government Accountability Office (GAO) shall establish baseline measurements of: (a) real after-tax median household income (source: Census Bureau / Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS)) and (b) median healthy life expectancy at birth (source: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention / National Center for Health Statistics (CDC/NCHS)).

#### SEC. 702. REVIEW CADENCE.
The GAO shall conduct biennial reviews of this Act's effectiveness. Every 2 years, the GAO shall re-measure both real after-tax median household income and median healthy life expectancy at birth, comparing the results to the established baselines and publicly publishing a comprehensive report.

#### SEC. 703. TARGET THRESHOLDS.
(a)  **Expected Improvements**: This Act targets the following improvements within 5 years of full implementation (Year 5):
    (1)  Real after-tax median household income: +0.5 percentage points (pp)/year growth, cumulative +2.5 pp over 5 years.
    (2)  Median healthy life expectancy at birth: +0.3 years within 5 years.
(b)  **Minimum Acceptable Thresholds**: The minimum acceptable thresholds for this Act's continuation are 50% of the expected improvements:
    (1)  Real after-tax median household income: +0.25 pp/year growth, cumulative +1.25 pp over 5 years.
    (2)  Median healthy life expectancy at birth: +0.15 years within 5 years.

#### SEC. 704. HARD SUNSET.
If, by the second biennial review (Year 4), **BOTH** the real after-tax median household income and median healthy life expectancy at birth fail to meet their respective MINIMUM acceptable thresholds as defined in Section 703(b), this Act shall automatically expire unless renewed by a supermajority vote (two-thirds majority) in both chambers of Congress.

#### SEC. 705. DATA COLLECTION MANDATE.
The following agencies are mandated to collect and publicly publish the necessary data for evaluation:
(a)  The Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) and the Census Bureau shall annually collect and publish data on real after-tax median household income.
(b)  The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) and the National Center for Health Statistics (NCHS) shall annually collect and publish data on median healthy life expectancy at birth.
(c)  The Office of Management and Budget (OMB) shall annually publish transparent, per-capita education spending data across all federal, state, and local sources, disaggregated by spending category.
(d)  All data collected and published under this section shall be made publicly accessible in easily digestible formats.

#### SEC. 706. BENCHMARK JURISDICTIONS.
The GAO shall, in its biennial reviews, explicitly benchmark the United States' performance on real after-tax median household income and median healthy life expectancy at birth against the following OECD countries: Japan, Singapore, Switzerland, and Sweden. If benchmark countries continue to outperform the United States on both metrics despite the reforms implemented under this Act, the review must include a detailed analysis explaining the reasons for the disparity and recommend specific adjustments to the Act's provisions.
