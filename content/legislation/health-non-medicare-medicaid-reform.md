### SECTION 3. PURPOSE.
The purpose of this Act is to establish a framework for transforming health (non-Medicare/Medicaid) spending in the United States, with the explicit goal of achieving health outcomes comparable to Japan (Life Expectancy: 84.53 years) while reducing non-Medicare/Medicaid per-capita spending from the current $10,333 toward the efficient floor of $3588. This will be accomplished by replacing inefficient existing systems with evidence-based approaches that leverage market mechanisms and universal coverage principles, and by reallocating the resulting savings directly to American citizens through an Optimization Dividend Pool.

---

### SECTION 4. KEY PROVISIONS.

#### Subtitle A — Universal Market-Based Health Financing System

**SEC. 401. ESTABLISHMENT OF THE NATIONAL HEALTH SAVINGS AND CATASTROPHIC COVERAGE SYSTEM.**

(a) **REPLACES:** Existing employer-sponsored health insurance mandates, individual market subsidies under the Affordable Care Act (ACA) for non-Medicare/Medicaid eligible individuals, and the fragmented private health insurance market for non-Medicare/Medicaid eligible individuals.
(b) **CURRENT COST:** Estimated at hundreds of billions annually through tax expenditures, subsidies, and out-of-pocket spending in the non-Medicare/Medicaid market.
(c) **NEW COST:** Cost of administering the new system, offset by mandatory savings and catastrophic insurance premiums.
(d) **NET SAVINGS:** Projected substantial net savings as outlined in Section 501.
(e) **SAVINGS DESTINATION:** All net savings flow to the Optimization Dividend Pool established in Section 601.

**SEC. 402. MANDATORY HEALTH SAVINGS ACCOUNTS (MEDISAVE-USA).**

(a) **WHAT IT DOES (MODELED ON SINGAPORE'S MEDISAVE):** Establishes mandatory, portable, tax-advantaged health savings accounts for all adult citizens and legal residents not covered by Medicare or Medicaid. These accounts are designed to cover routine medical expenses, preventive care, and co-payments, fostering individual responsibility and prudent healthcare consumption.
(b) **HOW IT WORKS OPERATIONALLY:**
    (1) **Contribution:** Employers and self-employed individuals shall contribute a mandatory percentage of wages (initially set at 7% for employees, with an equivalent contribution for the self-employed) into individual Medisave-USA accounts. This contribution shall be tax-deductible for employers and tax-exempt for employees.
    (2) **Usage:** Funds in Medisave-USA accounts may be used for approved outpatient services, prescription drugs, dental care, vision care, approved wellness programs, and premiums for MediShield-USA (catastrophic insurance).
    (3) **Portability:** Accounts are fully portable and owned by the individual, accumulating tax-free interest. Unused funds roll over year-to-year and can be used in retirement for medical expenses or transferred to beneficiaries upon death.

### SECTION 7. EVALUATION & SUNSET PROVISIONS.

**SEC. 701. BASELINE MEASUREMENT.**
(a) Within 90 days of the date of enactment of this Act, the Government Accountability Office (GAO) shall establish baseline measurements for:
    (1) Real after-tax median household income (source: U.S. Census Bureau and Bureau of Labor Statistics).
    (2) Median healthy life expectancy at birth (source: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention/National Center for Health Statistics) [6](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQGFUHneklE9NOC5KVn_2KgYTK6Je9dDDGUKlwMQ6kx9vsb5w9ENZ8q4G8fIYWTNp85ABIRqheVTid5frrKIKEAFD8sGggnNXZw72gxDqPaTZ13TYwWwjHDwHJtRPO8=).
(b) These two metrics, real after-tax median household income and median healthy life expectancy at birth, shall be the exclusive ultimate metrics for evaluating the success of this Act.

**SEC. 702. REVIEW CADENCE.**
(a) The GAO shall conduct a biennial review of the implementation and impact of this Act.
(b) Every two years following the establishment of the baseline, the GAO shall re-measure both the real after-tax median household income and median healthy life expectancy at birth, compare these measurements to the established baselines, and publish the results publicly.

**SEC. 703. TARGET THRESHOLDS.**
(a) The expected improvements from this Act are defined as:
    (1) A sustained increase of at least 0.5 percentage points per year in real after-tax median household income growth.
    (2) An increase of at least 0.3 years in median healthy life expectancy at birth within the first five years of full implementation.
(b) The minimum acceptable thresholds for this Act are defined as 50% of the expected improvements for both metrics.

**SEC. 704. HARD SUNSET.**
(a) If, by the second biennial review (Year 4 following the date of enactment), **BOTH** the real after-tax median household income and the median healthy life expectancy at birth fail to meet their respective minimum acceptable thresholds as defined in Section 703(b), then this Act shall automatically expire and be repealed, effective 180 days after the publication of the second biennial review, unless renewed by a supermajority vote (60 votes in the Senate, two-thirds vote in the House of Representatives) of both Houses of Congress.
(b) The purpose of this hard sunset provision is to prevent the continuation of ineffective legislation.

**SEC. 705. DATA COLLECTION MANDATE.**
(a) The Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) and the U.S. Census Bureau shall be mandated to collect and publish data on median after-tax household income annually.
(b) The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) and the National Center for Health Statistics (NCHS) shall be mandated to collect and publish data on median healthy life expectancy at birth annually.
(c) The Office of Management and Budget (OMB) shall be mandated to collect and publish data on per-capita health spending (excluding Medicare and Medicaid) annually, disaggregated by spending category.

**SEC. 802. CITIZEN CHOICE PROVISION.**
(a) Every citizen and legal resident receiving the Optimization Dividend shall have the option to redirect any portion of their monthly payment to any federal budget category of their choosing.
(b) This redirection shall be implemented via a secure, opt-in mechanism accessible through the TreasuryDirect website or a similar federal online portal.
