        (E) **SAVINGS DESTINATION:** All administrative net savings from replaced welfare programs shall flow to the "Optimization Dividend Pool." The direct payment portion is a redirection of existing welfare spending.
    (5) **PROJECTED PER-PERSON ANNUAL DIVIDEND (Initial calculation based on military savings alone).** Based on an estimated $557.1 billion in annual military savings and an adult U.S. population of approximately 258 million, the initial Optimization Dividend is projected to be approximately **$2159.92 per adult per year**, or approximately $180 per adult per month. Additional savings from healthcare and other reforms will increase this dividend.
    (6) **IMPLEMENTATION TIMELINE.**
        (A) **Year 1:** Identification and sunsetting of existing federal welfare programs, establishment of Optimization Dividend Pool infrastructure.
        (B) **Year 2:** Initial dividend distribution to all adult citizens. Establishment of Citizen Choice Portal.

#### SUBSECTION D. PRAGMATIC CLINICAL TRIALS FOR EVIDENCE-BASED MEDICINE ACT (Modeled on UK's NIHR).
    (1) **WHAT IT DOES.** This provision reforms federal funding for clinical trials to prioritize pragmatic, real-world studies focused on clinical and cost-effectiveness, modeled on the UK's National Institute for Health and Care Research (NIHR) approach. [3](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQHwlBaHpv8qM0wL4GlTT6LulHa-lJ7-dI5LaRKekrNAGb6aG6BoYIKBlgOf4gnjICDzLYGlA4qznxnfqXRtHJiUmR7OlTM9heIphHz4Tk1D6QmE9AMSCpFNEjmZ83nxnFySfYrWmH0M-PvOVZ0=) It aims to produce actionable evidence at a fraction of the current cost, ensuring taxpayer dollars fund research with direct patient benefit and value for money.
    (2) **HOW IT WORKS OPERATIONALLY.**
        (A) **NATIONAL INSTITUTE FOR HEALTH RESEARCH USA (NIHR USA).** The National Institutes of Health (NIH) shall establish a dedicated "NIHR USA" division to fund pragmatic randomized controlled trials (RCTs) directly relevant to common health conditions and healthcare delivery challenges in the U.S.
        (B) **COST-EFFECTIVENESS MANDATE.** All NIHR USA-funded trials shall explicitly incorporate cost-effectiveness analyses as a primary outcome, in addition to clinical efficacy, ensuring that public funds are directed towards interventions that provide the greatest health benefit for the cost. Adaptive trial designs will be encouraged to improve efficiency.
        (C) **PARTNERSHIPS FOR REAL-WORLD DATA.** NIHR USA shall foster partnerships with healthcare providers, patient advocacy groups, and the Medisave USA/MediShield Life USA system to embed research within routine clinical practice, leveraging real-world data for pragmatic study designs.
    (3) **EXPECTED IMPACT.**
        (A) **RESEARCH EFFICIENCY.** The UK's NIHR model is estimated to produce actionable evidence at 1/10th the cost of traditional US models.
        (B) **INTERMEDIATE METRIC: 50% reduction in average cost per actionable clinical trial.** (Measurable: NIHR USA annual reports compared to historical NIH data). This will free up research funds.
        (C) **INTERMEDIATE METRIC: 20% increase in the number of evidence-based guidelines adopted by healthcare providers within 5 years.** (Measurable: AHRQ National Healthcare Quality and Disparities Report). This leads to better patient care.
        (D) **ULTIMATE METRICS LINKAGE.** More efficient and impactful clinical trials lead to better and more affordable medical treatments, directly contributing to increased median healthy life expectancy and reducing healthcare costs, thus increasing after-tax median household income.
    (4) **REPLACES, CURRENT COST, NEW COST, NET SAVINGS, SAVINGS DESTINATION.**
        (A) **REPLACES:** A portion of the current NIH budget (estimated to be <10% on pragmatic trials, currently $48B/yr total NIH budget), redirecting funds from less pragmatic research to the NIHR USA model.
        (B) **CURRENT COST:** Approximately $4.8 billion annually (10% of $48 billion NIH budget).
        (C) **NEW COST (Projected):** Approximately $0.48 billion annually (1/10th of current pragmatic trial spending).
        (D) **NET SAVINGS:** Approximately $4.32 billion annually.
        (E) **SAVINGS DESTINATION:** All net savings shall flow to the "Optimization Dividend Pool."
    (5) **IMPLEMENTATION TIMELINE.**
        (A) **Year 1:** Establishment of NIHR USA within NIH, initial funding redirection.
        (B) **Year 2-5:** Phased transition of research priorities and funding models.

### SECTION 7. EVALUATION AND SUNSET PROVISIONS.

#### SECTION X01. BASELINE MEASUREMENT.
Within 90 days of enactment, the Government Accountability Office (GAO) shall establish and publicly report baseline measurements of:
    (a) Real after-tax median household income (source: U.S. Census Bureau, Current Population Survey Annual Social and Economic Supplement; Bureau of Labor Statistics).
    (b) Median healthy life expectancy at birth (source: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention/National Center for Health Statistics).

#### SECTION X02. REVIEW CADENCE.
The GAO shall conduct a biennial review, every two years following baseline measurement, to re-measure both metrics specified in Section X01 and compare them to the established baselines. The results of each review shall be published publicly.

#### SECTION X03. TARGET THRESHOLDS.
    (a) **EXPECTED IMPROVEMENTS.**
        (1) An average annual increase of at least 0.5 percentage points in real after-tax median household income growth over the baseline.
        (2) An average annual increase of at least 0.3 years in median healthy life expectancy at birth over the baseline.
    (b) **MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE THRESHOLDS.** If, at any review cadence, **both** metrics fail to meet 50% of the expected improvements outlined in Subsection (a) for the preceding two-year period, this shall constitute a critical failure of the Act's intended purpose.

#### SECTION X04. HARD SUNSET.
If, by the second biennial review (Year 4 following enactment), **both** the real after-tax median household income and the median healthy life expectancy at birth metrics fail to meet their respective minimum acceptable thresholds as defined in Section X03(b), this Act shall automatically expire and be repealed on January 1st of the subsequent year, unless renewed by a supermajority vote (60% of members present and voting) in both chambers of Congress.

#### SECTION X05. DATA COLLECTION MANDATE.
Federal agencies are hereby mandated to collect, process, and publish, in a timely and publicly accessible manner, the data necessary for the ongoing evaluation of this Act:
    (a) **Median Income:** U.S. Census Bureau (Current Population Survey Annual Social and Economic Supplement) and Bureau of Labor Statistics (Consumer Price Index for inflation adjustment) shall annually publish real after-tax median household income.
    (b) **Healthy Life Expectancy:** Centers for Disease Control and Prevention/National Center for Health Statistics shall annually publish median healthy life expectancy at birth.
    (c) **Per-Capita Spending:** The Office of Management and Budget shall annually publish detailed federal per-capita spending across all categories.
    (d) **Healthcare Procedure Costs:** The Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) shall annually publish average procedure costs for common medical services.
    (e) **Preventive Care Visits:** The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS) shall annually publish data on preventive care utilization.
    (f) **Preventable Hospitalizations:** The Agency for Healthcare Research and Quality (AHRQ) Prevention Quality Indicators (PQI) shall annually publish data on preventable hospitalizations.

#### SECTION X06. BENCHMARK JURISDICTIONS.
The GAO shall continuously monitor and report on the performance of the following OECD countries as ongoing benchmarks: Japan, Switzerland, Sweden, and Singapore. If, at any biennial review, these benchmark countries collectively continue to outperform the United States on both the real after-tax median household income and median healthy life expectancy at birth metrics, despite the reforms enacted by this Act, the GAO review shall include a detailed analysis explaining the reasons for the disparity and recommend specific legislative or administrative adjustments to further align U.S. performance with these exemplars.

#### SUBSECTION C. UNIVERSAL BASIC INCOME DIVIDEND FOR WELL-BEING (Alaska, Finland, GiveDirectly).
*   **Evidence:**
    *   **Alaska Permanent Fund Dividend:** Provides annual cash payments to all residents from oil revenues. Studies show it aids poverty reduction, particularly for vulnerable populations, and does not decrease aggregate employment; some evidence suggests it increases part-time work. Public support is widespread, with 81% of Alaskans stating it improves their quality of life.
    *   **Finland's UBI Pilot:** A two-year experiment providing unconditional monthly payments to unemployed individuals. Final results indicated a small, statistically significant positive impact on employment, and significantly boosted well-being, leading to less mental strain, depression, sadness, and loneliness, and improved perceived economic security and cognitive abilities.
    *   **GiveDirectly Cash Transfers:** Large-scale randomized controlled trials demonstrate that unconditional cash transfers improve food security, nutrition, savings, and empower recipients to invest in education, healthcare, and businesses. Critically, these studies found no evidence that cash transfers disincentivize work or increase spending on alcohol/tobacco. Long-term UBI and large lump sums proved more impactful for poverty reduction than short-term monthly payments.

#### SUBSECTION D. PRAGMATIC CLINICAL TRIALS FOR EVIDENCE-BASED MEDICINE ACT (UK NIHR Model).
*   **Evidence:** The UK's National Institute for Health and Care Research (NIHR) funds pragmatic randomized controlled trials (RCTs) within the National Health Service (NHS). This model prioritizes research questions of direct relevance to healthcare practice, evaluates both clinical and cost-effectiveness, and informs national policy decisions (e.g., NICE guidance). [3](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQHwlBaHpv8qM0wL4GlTT6LulHa-lJ7-dI5LaRKekrNAGb6aG6BoYIKBlgOf4gnjICDzLYGlA4qznxnfqXRtHJiUmR7OlTM9heIphHz4Tk1D6QmE9AMSCpFNEjmZ83nxnFySfYrWmH0M-PvOVZ0=) By focusing on real-world applicability and cost-efficiency, the NIHR model is capable of generating actionable evidence at a fraction of the cost of traditional, often less pragmatic, clinical research paradigms. The incorporation of cost-effectiveness criteria into trial design is a key differentiator.
